\section{Limitations}
\label{sec:limitations}

We acknowledge several limitations:

\paragraph{Scale and language.}
BiTempQA contains 308 items in Chinese only. While sufficient for diagnostic analysis (bootstrap CIs ${\leq}8$pp for major categories), small subcategories (record-only $n{=}24$, retraction $n{=}28$) have wide CIs that limit strong quantitative claims. Expanding to 600--1000 items and adding English translations are priorities for future work.

\paragraph{LLM-generated data.}
All QA pairs are generated by Qwen2.5-72B-Instruct and have not undergone human expert validation.
We mitigate this through: (1) structured generation with strict output schemas; (2) automated consistency checks; (3) LLM Judge cross-validation ($\kappa{=}0.455$).
However, some items may contain ambiguous or incorrectly specified answers.
Human validation on a stratified sample (100--150 items, 2 native annotators) is the single highest-priority next step.

\paragraph{ChromaDB confound.}
ChromaDB uses a different default embedding model (all-MiniLM-L6-v2, 22M parameters) than the other vector systems (SiliconFlow API, multilingual).
Its poor performance (41.2\%) likely reflects this configuration difference rather than fundamental limitations of persistent vector databases.
We include ChromaDB for completeness but exclude it from primary comparative claims.

\paragraph{Backend scope.}
We evaluate memory storage/retrieval backends rather than full agent memory systems (e.g., Mem0, Zep, Graphiti). These production systems incorporate additional components (memory consolidation, summarization, conflict resolution) that may improve bitemporal reasoning. Evaluating such systems is ongoing work.

\paragraph{LLM Judge leniency.}
DeepSeek-V3 is more lenient than Qwen2.5-72B (80\% vs.\ 60\% accuracy on a 50-item sample), with 11/12 disagreements favoring acceptance. Reported accuracies may be slightly inflated relative to a stricter judge, though relative rankings are preserved.
